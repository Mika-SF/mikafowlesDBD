Database Design and Normalisation

This unit focused on exploring database design and normalisation, with a particular emphasis on the construction and decomposition of a relational database. We examined how relevant information can be broken down into a normalised structure that facilitates the storage and retrieval of data.

Unit 6 covers:

  • Evaluating how the cleaning methods help with the storage of useable datasets.
  • Understanding how a database is created and how it is linked with the use of key fields.
  • Analysing anomalies and how they can affect the integrity of the database.
  • Looking at normalisation and the reasoning behind the use of different normal forms.

Learning Outcomes:

  • To discuss the construction and terminology associated with creating a database.
  • To identify technical terminology used in the construction of a relational database.
  • To become familiar with the process involved in creating a logical database design.
  • To evaluate the requirements for creating a physical architecture database design.
  • To analyse and evaluate the requirements and limitations for creating a physical design.
  • To understand the fundamentals of normalisation.
