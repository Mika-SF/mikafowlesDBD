Collaborative Discussion 1: The Data Collection Process - Initial Post

The Internet of Things (IoT) offers a multitude of opportunities to gain valuable insights into various aspects of people's behaviour, ranging from consumer behaviour to individual information such as personal health, by acquiring, storing, processing and analysing an enormous amount of data, often referred to as big data.

To obtain truly useful information from IoT data, it is crucial to employ a reliable, accurate and efficient system of data management, processing and analysis. However, the recent immense growth in multiple facets such as the amount of IoT data generated and the variation in types and formats of such data poses challenges for data science engineers and researchers (Cai et al., 2017). It is proposed that new architectures should cover IoT data characteristics including real-time event processing and data mining of rapidly changing, large and diverse data to extract meaningful and relevant information from the data stream (Cai et al., 2017). In this process, it is also essential to recognise particular aims and purposes of data analysis, as this would motivate us to understand what can be represented by missing values, errors and data anomalies, which would then allow us to determine how data should be cleaned (Huxley, 2020).

Furthermore, Talha et al. (2019) note that the volume and variety of data as well as the rapid speed that big data is generated give rise to a conflict between data quality and data security involving big data environments, suggesting that such a conflict should be taken into account for the appropriate and effective application of big data solutions.

References

Cai, H., Xu, B., Jiang, L. & Vasilakos, A. V. (2017). IoT-based big data storage systems in cloud computing: Perspectives and challenges. IEEE Internet of Things Journal, 4(1), 75-87.

Huxley, K. (2020) ‘Data Cleaning’, in Atkinson, P., Delamont, S., Cernat, A., Sakshaug, J.W., & Williams, R.A. (eds) Quantitative Data Preparation & Secondary Data Analysis. SAGE Research Methods Foundations. Available from: https://doi.org/10.4135/9781526421036842861 [Accessed 8th May 2023].

Talha, M., El Kalam, A. A. & Elmarzouqi, N. (2019). Big data: Trade-off between data quality and data security. Procedia Computer Science, 151, 916-922.
